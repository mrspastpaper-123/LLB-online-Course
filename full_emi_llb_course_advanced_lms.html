Weekly Course Material

Week 1: What is Law?

Law is a system of rules recognised and enforced by the state. It regulates behaviour, protects rights, resolves disputes and supports social order. Students learn the difference between law and morality, and why modern societies need predictable legal rules.

Learning Activity: Write a short answer explaining the key legal idea from this week. Then apply it to a simple real-life example.
Suggested Answer:
A strong answer should: (1) define the legal principle accurately; (2) explain why it is important; and (3) apply it to a real-life situation. For example: 'Negligence is a breach of a duty of care that causes damage. It is important because it protects people from avoidable harm. A driver who uses a mobile phone and causes an accident may be negligent.'
Case/Scenario Practice: A fictional client asks for advice. Identify the issue, rule, application and conclusion using IRAC.

Legal Glossary

TermMeaning
Actus ReusThe external or physical element of a crime.
Mens ReaThe mental element of a crime.
ClaimantA person who brings a civil claim.
DefendantA person against whom a claim or prosecution is brought.
PrecedentA legal decision that may guide future cases.
Ratio DecidendiThe legal reason for a court's decision.
Obiter DictaComments made by a judge that are not essential to the decision.
ConsiderationSomething of value exchanged in a contract.
NegligenceA tort involving breach of a duty of care causing damage.
TrustA legal relationship where one party holds property for another.
