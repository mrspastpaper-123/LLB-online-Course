Week 1: What is Law?

Self-Study Lesson Notes

What is Law? is an important part of Introduction to Legal Systems and Methods. In this week, students study the main legal idea, its purpose, and how it works in practical situations. Students should read the explanation slowly and identify the rule, the reason for the rule, and the type of factual problem where the rule may be used. A good law student should not only remember definitions. The student should ask: What legal issue is raised? What rule applies? What facts are important? What conclusion is most likely? This method helps students move from simple reading to legal reasoning. In English-medium legal study, students should also practise professional vocabulary. Write in clear complete sentences. Avoid emotional comments such as “this is obviously unfair” unless you can connect the point to a legal rule, authority or remedy. For self-study, read the weekly notes twice. On the first reading, understand the meaning. On the second reading, highlight key terms and write a short IRAC paragraph. Then complete the activity and case practice below.

  • What
  • Law?
  • legal rule
  • application
  • conclusion

Week 12: Final Presentation

Self-Study Lesson Notes

Final Presentation is an important part of Dissertation, Legal Clinic and Professional Ethics. In this week, students study the main legal idea, its purpose, and how it works in practical situations. Students should read the explanation slowly and identify the rule, the reason for the rule, and the type of factual problem where the rule may be used. A good law student should not only remember definitions. The student should ask: What legal issue is raised? What rule applies? What facts are important? What conclusion is most likely?
